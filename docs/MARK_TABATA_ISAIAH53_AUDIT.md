# Mark Tabata Isaiah 53 ELS Source Audit

Source: [The Mysterious Equidistant Letter Skipping Of Isaiah 53](https://marktabata.com/2017/04/11/the-mysterious-equidistant-letter-skipping-of-isaiah-53/),
Mark Tabata, April 11, 2017.

Status: public web-source audit and screening-list expansion. This is not a
reproduction report.

## Why This Source Matters

This article is a useful public-facing compilation of the Isaiah 53 ELS claim
family. It is not a primary dataset, but it gives a clean web citation for:

- the Isaiah 52:13-53:12 suffering-servant passage as the target surface span;
- the more-than-40-term crucifixion cluster popularized through Chuck Missler,
  Grant Jeffrey, and Yakov Rambsel material;
- the claim that isolated names are weak and that dense topical clustering in a
  short relevant passage is the asserted evidential feature;
- the Mary/John/Yeshua interweaving motif;
- WRR 1994 and Harold Gans as the article's supporting statistical context;
- an apologetic framing that treats ELS as possible confirming evidence, not a
  replacement for ordinary plaintext argument.

## Methodology Notes

This source reinforces project rules already used in the formal report:

- treat occurrence as a review fact but do not promote it without controls;
- separate isolated short-name frequency from declared cluster evidence;
- preserve source-specific spelling and attribution;
- audit exact letter paths before relying on a translated English term list;
- distinguish source-citation coverage from reproduction.

## Reproduction Requirements

Before elevating this source family:

1. lock the Hebrew source text edition for Isaiah 52:13-53:12;
2. recover exact Hebrew spellings from the cited Missler/Rambsel/Jeffrey sources;
3. lock each start reference, word index, letter index, skip, and direction;
4. model the Mary/John/Yeshua interweaving claim as geometry, not just counts;
5. run same-length passage-local real-word controls and non-Bible controls;
6. correct for all declared Isaiah 53 terms and any alternate spellings tested;
7. report version/source distribution across available MT-family texts.

## Cautions

- The article is a secondary web compilation.
- The article mostly gives English labels rather than exact Hebrew strings.
- Several terms are short common names and need especially strong controls.
- The statistical discussion summarizes WRR/Gans claims but does not define a
  complete reproducible statistic for the Isaiah 53 list.
- These additions expand the audit surface; they do not validate the claims.
