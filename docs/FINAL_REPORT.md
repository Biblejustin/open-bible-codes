Status: reader-facing report over the current locked report set. This is not a

Primary assembly source: `reports/real_report_run/summary.md`, generated by

## Executive Summary

This project tests whether equidistant letter sequences can be found and
measured in biblical texts across Hebrew, Greek, and English corpora. It
searches normalized continuous letter streams with forward and backward signed
skips, records source/version presence, tracks center words and span context,
checks same-skip before/after extensions, and compares results against matched
controls, shuffled controls, and non-Bible control texts.

The current result is cautious:

- hidden paths exist;
- centered-self and relevant-center occurrences exist and should be listed;
- several rows deserve manual review;
- no current result is claim-level;
- short terms and wider skip ranges create large background hit rates;
- controls often explain rows that look striking before comparison.

The best current public wording is: review candidates and occurrence findings,
not proof.

## Hypothesis Scope

The working hypothesis is that original-language biblical texts may contain
letter-level patterns detectable by ELS search. Hebrew and Greek biblical texts
are the primary target. English translations, including KJV, are secondary
screens. A pattern does not have to appear in every version to matter; the
useful question is which pattern appears in which witness, with which spelling,
skip, direction, center, and control background.

The report keeps three layers separate:

- occurrence/context: whether a hidden term appears and where it centers;
- source distribution: which textual witnesses contain the same pattern;
- statistical background: whether matched controls make the row unusual.

Report convention: Hebrew and Greek terms are shown with transliteration and an
English gloss where available.

### KJVA Apocrypha/Deuterocanon Bridge

The KJV + Apocrypha bridge lane is the strongest apocrypha/deuterocanon
follow-up set so far. The bridge pass found 350 boundary-spanning rows. The
expanded 250-sample shuffled-insertion control produced 149 to 236 bridge rows,
with no shuffled sample at or above the observed 350 (`p_ge=0.003984`).

The 1000-sample term-level shuffled control found 15 terms with
Benjamini-Hochberg `q_ge <= 0.05`. The locked 5000-sample post-screen
confirmatory follow-up over those 15 terms found all 15 with `q_ge <= 0.01`,
and 3 terms stood above every shuffled sample.

This raises follow-up priority, but it remains post-screen rather than
claim-level evidence.

## Negative And Weak Results

### Gog/Magog Hebrew Pair Lane

The locked Hebrew Gog/Magog prospective pair-control study completed over
MT_WLC and UHB. Target rows occurred in both sources, but both were
`not_unusual` under pair controls, and synthetic length-matched 3+4 Hebrew
pairs often matched or exceeded the target close-pair density.

Result: no `prospective_controlled_review_candidate`.

Source artifact: `docs/GOG_MAGOG_PAIR_PROSPECTIVE_REPORT.md`.

### Modern, Geopolitical, And Local Terms

Modern and local terms remain weak overall. Short forms such as Trump, Vance,
Iran, USA, and similar abbreviations can appear because short forms are dense.
Longer phrases such as United States, United States of America, United Nations,
European Union, Cowboy Catering, Catering, and Simscorner remain absent or
effectively absent in the observed Hebrew and Greek screens.

The locked Hebrew modern/geopolitical prospective follow-up had:

- 72 rows entering the min-length endpoint;
- 45 rows with all-source exact patterns;
- 27 rows absent in the capped exact-version screen;
- 2 rows as uncorrected-only prompts;
- 0 rows surviving BH-adjusted representative controls.

Result: source-distribution review material only.

Source artifacts:

- `docs/HEBREW_MODERN_GEOPOLITICAL_PROSPECTIVE_REPORT.md`

### Full-Span Skip Expansion

Full-span search collects very large hit sets. The dense export summarized
13,556,483,793 exported full-span hit rows from 147 dense corpus/term rows.
That confirms the tool can collect broad-hit material, but it also confirms the
statistical problem: raw hit volume grows with skip cap and term density.

Raw counts alone should not be read as evidence of meaning.

### ChurchAges-Style Raw Count Claims

The ChurchAges-style statistics audit found raw counts close to
independent-letter density expectations:

- `branham` forward: observed 655 vs expected 639.705853;
- `branham` backward: observed 612 vs expected 639.705853;
- `statist` forward: observed 11,947 vs expected 12,298.959594.

This addresses raw count-density claims. It does not address theology or
post-hoc cluster selection.

### WRR 1994

WRR 1994 has not yet been reproduced. The current repo imports source files,
audits source shape, counts terms in Koren Genesis, and documents methodology
gaps. It does not yet implement the full corrected distance `c(w,w')`, the
locked pair eligibility path, term-specific skip caps, or the permutation-rank
procedure needed for a claim-grade WRR reproduction.

## Bottom Line

The current project has real, reproducible occurrence and review-candidate
material. The most important finding is not that every striking row is
meaningful. It is that the toolkit can collect the rows, preserve their
source/version/context details, run controls, and keep occurrence findings
separate from claim language.

At this point, no current row should be presented as proof. The proper next
step is a narrower locked prospective study with fixed terms, fixed corpora,
fixed skip rules, fixed center/extension rules, and fixed controls before
seeing new result-producing output.
