# Religions Wiki Scriptural Codes Source Audit

Source: [Argument from scriptural codes](https://religions.wiki/index.php/Argument_from_scriptural_codes),
Religions Wiki. The page reports last edit date November 14, 2020, and states
that content is available under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 2.5.

Status: public critique and methodology audit. This is not a reproduction
report.

## Why This Source Matters

This page is useful because it classifies scriptural-code arguments more
broadly than Bible-only ELS sources:

- Torah/Bible ELS and WRR 1994;
- Qur'an ELS and the "miracle of 19";
- Bible numerics and sevenfold patterns;
- gematria and theomatics;
- Chinese-character arguments connected to Genesis themes;
- non-Bible controls such as `War and Peace` and `Moby Dick`;
- multiple comparisons, Texas sharpshooter fallacy, apophenia, and cognitive
  bias as methodological objections;
- source-corruption/source-variation objections;
- expert rejection and later statistical critique of the WRR paper.

## Methodology Notes

This source reinforces these project rules:

- report the tested universe, not only surprising examples;
- correct for multiple comparisons across term choices, skip ranges, versions,
  and layouts;
- run secular/non-Bible controls before making source-specific claims;
- separate ELS, numerics, gematria, theomatics, acrostics, and pictographic
  claims into different methods;
- treat source-version instability as a central claim variable;
- avoid "secretive God" or apologetic interpretation language in empirical
  report rows.

## Reproduction Requirements

Before elevating any row from this source:

1. lock the primary source behind the cited claim, not just the wiki summary;
2. define the exact method family being tested;
3. declare all texts, versions, terms, skip ranges, layouts, and numerical
   transforms;
4. include non-Bible controls where the source specifically raises them;
5. use family-level correction across all tested rows;
6. keep critique examples and positive claim examples in separate catalog rows.

## Cautions

- This is a wiki critique page, not a primary statistical paper.
- It summarizes several linked sources that need independent audit if used as
  primary evidence.
- It includes non-Bible and non-ELS examples; those should not be forced into
  ELS-only reports.
- These additions expand the audit and control surface; they do not validate or
  invalidate any claim by themselves.
